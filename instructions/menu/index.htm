Menu Interface



This is the Main Menu. From here, you can view your characters' statistics, use items, equip weapons, clothing, and accessories, and save or end your game. Check the number below to identify what each part is.

1. Item - Takes you to the Item menu.

2. Tech Skill - Press the confirm button on this, then select a character to see his or her
Tech Skills. Tech Skills with dimmed names cannot be used outside of
battle. Any Tech Skill whose name is not dimmed can be used, as long
as that character has enough MP to use it.

3. Equipment - Takes you to the Equipment menu, where you can change what weapons
your characters have equipped and what each of them is wearing. Some
things can only be equipped on a certain character, while other things
can be equipped on any character. To replace a piece of a character's
equipment, highlight the piece of equipment you want to replace, then
press the confirm button. You can then highlight the piece of equipment
you want to replace the old thing with. Press the confirm button, and the
new equipment will be equipped on the character.

4. Save - Take you to the save screen, where you can save your game. Highlight the file
you want to save to, and press the confirm button to save. You will
automatically be returned to the Main Menu.

5. End Game - Highlight this and press the confirm button to end your current game.

6. Money - This shows your current amount of money.

7. Mugshot - A picture of the character's face.

8. Name - The character's name.

9. Level - The character's current level.

10. Status Condition - Shows if the character is being affected by any bad conditions, i.e. malicious spells.

11. Class - Tells the character type.

12. HP - The number on the left is the current Hit Points (HP) of the character, and the number on the
right is the maximum HP of that character. Hit Points are the overall strength of the character.

13. MP - The number on the left is the current Magic Points (MP) of the character, and the number on
the right is the maximum MP of that character. Magic Points represent the ability of the character to use special skills.

14. Experience Points - The number on the left is the total number of Experience Points
that the character has. The number on the right is the number of
Experience Points that the character must reach to gain a level.
